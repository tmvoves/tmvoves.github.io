tips

Skip the flowers

In the fall and winter, cover surfaces with fresh greens like eucalyptus or pine. Or create piles of apples or clementines for centerpieces, and people can eat them if they want.

Don't forget
the ice

 Use silicone ice molds for mixed drinks—they create bigger ice cubes that melt slowly, so your drink doesn't get as watered-down. For punches, make an ice mold. It's easy: Fill a Bundt pan with water and freeze it. Run the bottom under hot water to loosen the ice and place it in your serving bowl to keep the drink cool without diluting it.

Stock up on Basics

A bottle each of gin, vodka, whiskey and bourbon, classic mixers like club soda and tonic, and pre-sliced lemons and limes. Also, keep a few six-packs of beer in the fridge—something light like Stella, plus a pumpkin lager for fall—as well as a few non-alcoholic options like good ginger ale or soda.
