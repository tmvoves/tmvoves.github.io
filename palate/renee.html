Renee Erickson

Renee Erickson is a James Beard award winning chef and owner of Sea Creatures Concept’s. Her food is driven by her love for the local ingredients in the Pacific Northwest

Arguably the best oyster bar in all America (it was a BA Hot 10 Restaurant in 2011), it’s got fresh, expertly shucked bivalves; small plates like smoked herring croquettes; creative cocktails; and a charming marble-topped bar you won’t want to leave.

Erickson’s airy, light-filled sophomore effort (another Hot 10 winner in 2013) feels like a sleek seaside cottage—albeit with a wood-burning oven and tons of vegetables. The seasonal salads (hello parsnip, hazelnut, capers, and crispy ham) and other share-plates make for healthy-ish grazing.
Most use this narrow 13-seat aperitivo bar attached to Walrus to drink their waits away, but it’s also a destination unto itself. Make like you’re in Italy and snack on hand-shaved Serrano ham, tender octopus terrine, and free potato chips while sipping sherry-and-limoncello drinks.
Named after a mythical mermaid­like creature (yep, the same one on your Starbucks cup), it’s Erickson’s ode to the French Atlantic. That means plenty of oysters on the half shell and a soon-to-be-famous crepe. The pro move is to grab starters and a bottle of Muscadet, then move on to Bateau.
Erickson comes ashore with this steakhouse-style spot refreshingly free of boys’-club clichés. Look to the chalkboard for lesser-known cuts like coulotte, tip sirloin, and gracilis—all butchered in-house from cattle raised on Erickson’s farm on nearby Whidbey Island. The burger’s great, too!
Really good doughnuts (the yeast variety, made using French eggs) and really good coffee (from top roasters across the country) in a cute take-out space next to Bateau. The fillings for the doughnuts change seasonally, but expect options like chocolate marshmallow and white nectarine jam.
